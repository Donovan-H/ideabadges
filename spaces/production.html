Production Equipment

A maker space can be described as a workshop. This workshop gives its users access to a wide range of digital fabrication machines and tools!
Choosing the right type of CAD software and then production equipment to create your product is an very important set of decisions.

Let's look at some examples of machines that can be found in a typical maker space. Find all 6!



40W LASER CUTTER

40W laser cutter – uses a powerful laser to cut materials such as acrylic, thin wood, cardboard, paper and cloth. Not only can it cut, but it can etch into the materials, which you can use to create personalised messages, patterns and designs. This piece of machinery works from 2D design files and is not capable of producing 3D shapes.

3D PRINTER

3D printer – works from 3D design files which produces plastic models that can be printed with any shape or direction, this allows for complex designs to be made including those with moving parts.

CNC ROUTER

CNC router – works from 2D and 3D design files and is basically a bigger version of the micro mill machine. This is mainly used for producing large items such as furniture, 3D mock ups of products, large signs, storage units, beds and chairs.

MICRO MILL MACHINE

Micro mill machine – works from 2D or 3D files and is mainly used in the workshop for the production of circuit boards by milling through a copper coated fibre board.

VINYL CUTTER

Vinyl cutter – works from 2D design files and is used for producing items such as small signs, notices, stickers, decals and designs for T-shirts that you can heat print.

ELECTRONICS

Electronics – this is used for soldering, programming and testing electronic products. To understand electronics, it requires the knowledge of at least one programming language. No design software is needed in electronics.
